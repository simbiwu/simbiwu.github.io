Privacy Notice

Last updated: July 23, 2026

SuperWatchDog is a local Windows privacy protection tool. Its core features are built around local vault files, trusted device triggers, and away protection actions. This notice explains the privacy boundaries for the website, installer, and Windows client.

Scope

This notice applies to the SuperWatchDog website, installer, and Windows client features related to personal privacy protection. If future versions add account services, cloud services, or new online capabilities, those will require additional notice.

What We Do Not Collect

  • We do not upload your files, file lists, vault contents, passwords, recovery keys, or personal documents.
  • We do not save plaintext BitLocker passwords or store your recovery materials for you.
  • We do not upload camera frames, screen captures, window contents, or local file path lists.
  • The website does not embed third-party analytics, advertising scripts, tracking scripts, or remote fonts.

Local Processing

Vault operations, trusted device state, and protection policy actions are designed to be processed locally on your Windows PC. Basic protection features do not depend on a remote account or cloud service.

Local Logs and Diagnostics

The software may write essential local status, error, and diagnostic logs. After an unexpected exit, it may also save an error code, app version, redacted logs, and a limited number of small crash dumps locally.

A diagnostic report is sent or exported only after you confirm. Small crash dumps are excluded by default and require a separate selection; a dump may contain a small amount of memory near the failing thread. Reports do not collect vault contents, file lists, passwords, recovery keys, camera frames, screenshots, or window contents.

Downloads and Updates

The website may provide installer download links. When downloading installer packages, your browser communicates with the relevant download host. Online update checks use a release index and installer hash verification before launching the installer.

Timed License Time Check

Free and permanent licenses do not perform license time checks. A timed Pro license periodically retrieves the current UTC time over an encrypted connection to evaluate its term. The request does not send a machine code, license ID, vault information, device list, or operational metrics. If the official domain is temporarily unavailable, the software may use the encrypted IP address of the same official server.

Anonymous Operational Data

Release builds may submit a random installation identifier, app version, release channel, and app-start event to measure installations, activity trends, and version distribution. The identifier is not derived from the machine GUID, hardware serial number, or Windows account. No vault information, device list, file path, or camera frame is included. Submission uses encrypted connections only, and failure does not affect product features.

Your Responsibility

Keep your vault files, BitLocker password, and recovery materials safe. If you forget the password and lose recovery materials, vault contents may be unrecoverable. SuperWatchDog does not save plaintext BitLocker passwords and cannot decrypt a vault for you.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact simbi@live.com.
