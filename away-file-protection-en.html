Scenario guide · Away protection

How to protect files and the screen after you leave a PC

Short periods away are common in offices, dorms, and shared workspaces. The goal is not to enable every detection method, but to choose a trigger that matches the devices you carry, your routine, and the PC environment.

Different ways of leaving fit different triggers

Away scenarioPossible methodMain difference
You carry a phone or headphonesBluetooth disconnectResponds to a linked Bluetooth device and can help when you forget to act first.
You take a USB device with youUSB device removalUses an explicit unplug action, making the trigger moment easier to anticipate.
You often remember only after standing upCamera-based away detectionChecks whether someone is at the PC and starts protection after configured conditions are met.
You know you are about to leaveOne-click or keyboard actionStarts directly before departure without waiting for a device-state change.

File protection and screen protection serve different roles

File vault

Keeps selected local documents together for ongoing protection. Files can remain locked while a PC is shared, lent, or serviced.

Away-triggered protection

Handles the immediate state around a departure. After a trigger, the app follows the protection choices already made by the user.

What to consider before choosing an automatic trigger

  • Bluetooth fits people who carry a phone, headphones, or watch, but connection behavior may vary with distance and surroundings.
  • USB removal is an explicit action, so its trigger timing is generally easier to predict.
  • Away detection helps when operating the PC is inconvenient or easy to forget, but it requires an available camera and must be enabled by the user.
  • Camera detection is processed in real time on the PC; frames are not saved, recorded, or uploaded.

Automatic triggers can reduce missed actions, but they do not replace backups, password care, or good device habits. Start with one method, try it in your environment, and add another only when needed.
